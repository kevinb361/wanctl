<objective>
Turn the 225-01 trace evidence and 225-02 ingress-DSCP distribution into a single gated DSCP-03
verdict that either short-circuits the A/B (marks don't survive → v1.44 confirmed → milestone may
close negative) or unblocks Phase 226 (marks survive → proceed). Compute the verdict against the

</objective>

<context>
The decision logic is pre-registered in `225-RESEARCH.md` (## DSCP-03), hardened after cycle-1 and
cycle-2 reviews: thresholds are numeric, the branches are mutually exclusive and evaluated
top-to-bottom, and `unknown`/`invalid` GATING channels ALWAYS map to the QUALIFIED fail-safe (never
to the negative branch).

**Gating vs corroborating channels (cycle-2 HIGH-A).** Only `organic_dl_histogram` and `dl_ef_probe`
GATE the DL diffserv verdict. `bridge_counter_signal` and `ul_ef_probe` are corroborating-only /
non-gating — their state (including `unknown`) never gates a branch. The production `ip dscp set`
rules carry no `counter` clause, so `bridge_counter_signal=unknown` is the EXPECTED benign case and
cannot force or block any branch; `organic_dl_histogram` (the pre-wash DSCP histogram) is the
authoritative DL marking signal. This makes the counters-absent case deterministic.

MARKS_DO_NOT_SURVIVE fires only on a logical AND of two VALID negatives: NEGLIGIBLE DL histogram
(non-BestEffort packets <1.0% AND non-BestEffort bytes <1.0% where measurable — both `NONBE_PKT_PCT`
and `NONBE_BYTE_PCT`) AND DL EF probe STRIPPED (<10%), both gating DL channels `valid` and above the
sample floor. MARKS_SURVIVE fires on MEANINGFUL DL marking (≥5.0%) OR a surviving DL EF probe (≥90%).
Everything else — ambiguous bands, degraded probes, unproven wash ordering / `CAPTURE_POINT=unknown`,
quiet windows, UL-only positives — is MARKS_SURVIVE_QUALIFIED.

**QUALIFIED blocks Phase 226 (cycle-2 HIGH-B).** ROADMAP.md gates Phase 226 on "marks survive."
QUALIFIED is NOT "marks survive" — it is a HOLD state. It does NOT auto-unblock Phase 226 (which
builds Snapshot A and touches production config) and does NOT auto-close the milestone negative. The
verdict must surface a REQUIRED operator decision: collect better evidence and re-run, OR an explicit
recorded operator override to proceed-with-caveat (deferring the volume question to Phase 226's
GATE-01 tie-breaker). The verdict logic never takes that override automatically.

The SAFE-13 boundary check must match the FULL v1.48 Phase 224 (SAFE-12) standard, NOT the weaker
`git diff --name-only` the original draft used (which misses staged/unstaged/untracked state). The
reference artifact is the v1.48 `safe12-boundary-check.json`, which recorded `per_path_diff` (per-file
added/removed/lines), `per_file_sha256_equal`, `dirty_tree` (unstaged/staged/untracked/status_porcelain),
`dirty_tree_clean`, `committed_clean`, and commit anchors. Phase 225 is trace/evidence + scripts only,
so SAFE-13 is expected to hold trivially; the value is the recorded proof at the SAFE-12 standard.
</context>
