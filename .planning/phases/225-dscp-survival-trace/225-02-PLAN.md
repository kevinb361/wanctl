<objective>
Capture the actual DSCP distribution arriving at the Spectrum CAKE enqueue interfaces under both
representative organic traffic and a deliberately EF-marked probe flow, satisfying DSCP-02. Because
production CAKE runs `besteffort` (single tin), per-tin CAKE counters cannot answer survival â€” so
observe the DSCP byte directly at the egress interface via bounded `tcpdump`, histogrammed offline,
read-only. Pair the organic distribution with a known EF probe to prove whether a specific mark
survives to the shaper.
</objective>

<context>
DL CAKE egress is `spec-router`, UL CAKE egress is `spec-modem`, both on host `cake-shaper`
(10.10.110.223). The bridge `spectrum_dl` chain marks DL flows just upstream of `spec-router`; the
question is whether those marks (and endpoint EF marks) reach the enqueue point intact. `tcpdump`
on the egress interface reading `ip[1] & 0xfc` (the DSCP bits of the TOS byte) gives a read-only
per-DSCP histogram without touching the qdisc filter list. A low-rate EF probe (`iperf3 --tos 0xb8`
or `nping --tos 0xb8`, DSCP 46) injected and observed at the same interface tells us whether a
known mark survives. The EF probe should originate from cake-shaper for the UL clean-mark case
(guaranteed markable) and attempt a LAN-client DL probe best-effort (WMM may strip). The read-only
constraint forbids gear-config mutation and CAKE-mode change â€” it does not forbid benign bounded
diagnostic traffic, consistent with prior flent/RRUL capture phases.
</context>
