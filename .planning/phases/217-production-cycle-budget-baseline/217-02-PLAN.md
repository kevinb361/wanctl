## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-217-03 | Denial of service | wanctl@spectrum control loop | mitigate | DEBUG adds ~240 log writes/sec; WatchdogSec=30s could restart the loop if I/O stalls it. Operator tails `journalctl -u wanctl@spectrum -f` during the window; on overrun/restart, abort `--debug` and fall back to `--profile` + `/health` polling. Capture is time-bounded and reverted. |
| T-217-04 | Elevation / persistence | systemd drop-in left enabled | mitigate | The drop-in is the only persistent state. Mandatory `systemctl revert` + verify `systemctl cat` shows no `override.conf` AND running ExecStart has no `--profile`/`--debug`. This is the phase's single most important safety gate (D-05). |
| T-217-05 | Information disclosure | .planning/perf/spectrum-capture-raw.log + health snapshot committed to repo | mitigate | The raw DEBUG log and `/health` snapshot may carry router IPs / operating points. Per CLAUDE.md public-safe policy, the committed artifacts are inspected for secrets/credentials before commit; non-secret operating points are acceptable evidence (Phase 212/213 precedent), but no secrets, keys, or `/etc/wanctl/secrets` content may be present. Final scrub/verdict is enforced at the Plan 03 commit gate. |

No HIGH threats remain mitigated-to-acceptable: the capture is transient, watchdog-supervised, reverted-and-verified, and the only data leaving the host is a profiling log scrubbed before commit. The DoS surface is bounded by the watchdog and the operator-monitored fallback.
